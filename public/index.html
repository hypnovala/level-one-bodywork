About Brock

Nervous system educator & somatic bodywork practitioner.

Brock John is a somatic bodywork practitioner and nervous system educator based in Houston, TX. His practice draws from therapeutic touch, somatic awareness training, and polyvagal-informed education.

Brock works with high-stress professionals, caregivers, and people navigating chronic tension — helping them rebuild a felt sense of safety and ease in the body. His approach is calm, grounded, and rooted in the idea that the body already knows how to heal when given the right conditions.

Through somatic awareness education and therapeutic touch, clients restore nervous system balance and recover from the chronic stress that accumulates over years of high-demand living.

Why this exists

Your body is not broken.

Long hours, emotional load, caregiving roles, and constant responsibility can keep the nervous system in an extended stress response. Over time, the body may stay activated long after the day is over.

This shows up as chronic tension, poor sleep, difficulty relaxing, emotional exhaustion, and a sense of disconnection from your own body. That is biology — not weakness.

The problem
Why people feel stuck

Many people end up feeling wired, tense, and disconnected — unable to truly relax.

Disconnection is often the nervous system adapting to overload. It is not a character flaw. It is a patterned response that, with steady and gentle support, can be retrained.

Key reframe

The goal is not force. The goal is gentle retraining.

Repeated experiences of awareness, safety, and regulation help the body relearn calm over time. This is what Level One Bodywork is built around.

Try this 60-second practice

Body Awareness Reset

  1. Sit comfortably and close your eyes.
  2. Take three slow breaths, letting each exhale be longer than the inhale.
  3. Notice where your body feels tight, heavy, or held.
  4. Observe the sensation without trying to change it.

The goal is awareness, not fixing. Regulation is a skill the body can learn again.

FAQ

Common questions

What is included inside the membership?
Members receive the Brock Check-In App, Brock Book Guide (BBG) access, monthly 1-on-1 somatic exercise calls, Nervous System Reset Worksheets, and priority access to in-person Houston bodywork sessions.
Is this medical care or therapy?
No. This is educational wellness support focused on somatic awareness, nervous system literacy, and practical regulation tools. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, or therapy.
Who is this designed for?
Level One Bodywork is designed for people in high-stress roles — nurses, healthcare workers, teachers, caregivers, and anyone who feels chronically tense, wired, numb, or emotionally exhausted and wants practical support.
What does the Check-In App do?
The Check-In App is a guided daily self-assessment that takes 3–5 minutes. It helps you track your nervous system state over time and identify patterns of activation or shutdown. Brock reviews your entries before each 1:1 call.
Can I cancel any time?
Yes. Membership is month-to-month and can be cancelled any time with 24-hour notice before your next renewal date. No commitments required.
How long does Level One take?
Level One training varies per woman, but most members move through it in 3 to 6 months. There is no fixed endpoint — progress is determined by the signals and patterns your body shows over time, not a countdown clock.

Some women arrive carrying years of chronic tension and may spend closer to 6 months building a consistent regulation foundation. Others shift more quickly and are ready for deeper work sooner. Both are normal. The 1:1 calls and Check-In App data help Brock track your progress and let you know when your body is genuinely ready to move forward.
What is the Brock Book Guide?
The BBG is a structured coaching tool that uses your current book as a reflection anchor. You receive a guided worksheet for that title, then join a 30-minute 1:1 call with Brock to work through the insights — connecting what you're reading to what your body is experiencing.
Why is there a 30-day waiting period for bodywork?
The 30-day period is not a gatekeeping measure — it is preparation. Before your first session, Brock uses your Check-In App data, worksheets, and 1:1 calls to understand the specific signals and patterns of your nervous system. That way, your first bodywork session is not generic. It is built around what your body has actually been communicating for the past month. Members who go through this process consistently report sessions that feel far more targeted and effective than walk-in bodywork.
What kind of bodywork does Level One use — and what does it not use?
Level One bodywork is designed to guide the body into a parasympathetic (rest and restore) state. That means sessions focus on methods that calm and relax the nervous system — therapeutic stretching, breathwork, full-body relaxation massage, and polarity therapy.

What Level One does not use: deep tissue work, stimulating pressure, or techniques designed to excite or activate the body. The goal is the opposite — to help the body experience safety, let go of held tension, and practice genuine rest. This distinction matters because many people in chronic stress have never received bodywork calibrated to their actual nervous system state.
